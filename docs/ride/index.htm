ride 2005

the ride is over!

why would i want to ride my bike, all alone through the most remote places in the country, for 40-plus miles a day for two-and-a-half months straight? i dont know. just sounds like fun.

once the ride starts, this site will chronicle my journey. it will have maps showing my progress and how much of the ride remains. it will have a trip log including pictures i take along the way. and it will have a way for you to send me a quick message to check in on me, give me some encouragement, or remind me how crazy you think i am for doing this.

vital stats:
- start in Port of Rooseville, Montana on August 1st
- travel through 5 states (Montana, Idaho, Wyoming, Colorado, New Mexico)
- 2,490 miles on dirt roads, jeep trails, singletrack, and pavement
- 27 crossings of the Continental Divide
- end in Antelope Wells, New Mexico in mid October
- lowest point: 2,577 feet (near the Canadian border)
- highest point: 11,910 (Indian Pass, Colorado)
- over 200,000 feet of elevation gain
- 62 days (not counting rest days)
- average 40+ miles a day
